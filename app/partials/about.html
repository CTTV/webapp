About the CTTV target validation platform

The CTTV platform brings together information on the relationships between potential drug targets and diseases. The core concept is to identify evidence of an association between a target and disease from various data types. A target can be a protein, protein complex or RNA molecule, but we integrate evidence through the gene that codes for the target. In the same way, we describe diseases through a structure of relationships called the Experimental Factor Ontology (EFO ) that allows us to bring together evidence across different but related diseases.

The platform supports workflows starting from either a target or disease and presents the evidence for target – disease associations in a number of ways through association and evidence pages. We also present related information on the target or the disease itself through the target profile and disease profile pages.

Search box

To find a target of interest, either enter the gene or protein name, symbol or synonym. To find a disease you can search for the common disease name or its synonyms (including some abbreviations) provided the disease label and its synonyms are part of the EFO .

In both scenarios, the platform will present a selection of up to 3 possible matches for targets and disease as shortcuts as you type, which can be selected by clicking on the names as they are presented. Clicking on a target symbol links to the Target Association page for that target. To get a fuller search, click the search icon or hit return and an exhaustive search result will be presented.

Results of a search are presented by order of relevance based on the known synonyms, matching of the symbol, names or synonym. Matched terms are highlighted.

The search gives results for both target and disease together, but the search results can be filtered by target and/or disease by selecting the filters at the top left. Number of matching targets and number of matching diseases are shown on the left-hand panel.

Target Association page

This provides an overview of all diseases associated to a target.

3 views are available: a Bubble view, a Table view and a Tree view.

Mouse over a bubble or a tree node to display the full disease name and the association score.

Clicking on a Bubble node, Tree node or a Table row label will open a pop-up with several links. Click on ‘View evidence details’ to view detailed Target-Disease Evidence page for the association, click on the ‘P’ icon to view the Disease Profile page or the ‘A’ icon to view all the target associations of that disease.

Filters are available on the left of the page to restrict the associations that are displayed by score, data type or to focus on a therapeutic area.

Association strength filter

Target associations can be filtered by strength from the lowest evidence-supported association strength ('low') to the strongest evidence-supported association strength ('high'). Clicking on any of the proposed options ('none', 'low', 'medium' or 'high') will change the number of diseases reported and will update the data distribution histogram accordingly.

  • By default, all the association between a target a set of diseases are shown on the Target Association page ('none'). It's reflected in the association strength panel with the 'none' radio button selected by default along with the data distribution histogram of all diseases binned by association score.
  • Clicking on the 'low' radio button will narrow the score range of associations and exclude the associations with a score lower than 0.2. This is reflected in the data distribution histogram where the lower bin (0.0-0.2) is in grey.
  • Clicking on the 'medium' radio button will narrow the score range of associations and exclude the associations with a score lower than 0.6. The overall score of an association between a target and a disease is rescaled by dividing by 3 each datatype score to report the strongest associations. This is reflected in the data distribution histogram where the score bin range (0.8-1.0) is highlighted and the new distribution of the disease association score.
  • Clicking on the 'high' radio button will narrow the score range of associations and exclude the associations with a score lower than 0.8. The overall score of an association between a target and a disease is also rescaled by dividing by 5 each datatype score to report the strongest associations. This is reflected in the data distribution histogram where the score bins range (0.8-1.0) is highlighted and the new distribution of the disease association score.

Data distribution

By default and in combination with the Association strengh filter, the score distribution of all the disease associated with a target is displayed as an histogram.

Any change in the Association strength filter will be reflected in the histogram.

'Data types' filters

The diseases displayed in these views can be restricted by the data types for which they have evidence. The number of associated diseases is indicated next to each data type. Clicking/unclicking on a data type filter updates the Bubble, Table or Tree representation accordingly.

Bubble View

This view groups diseases into bubbles by therapeutic area. A disease can belong to multiple therapeutic areas and therefore can be in more than one bubble. To zoom in on a particular therapeutic area, click on the corresponding therapeutic area filter on the left hand side. To zoom out of a particular therapeutic area, click on the therapeutic area filter again. Hovering over a bubble shows the association score between a specific disease and a target.

Table View

This view lists all diseases associated to a particular gene ordered by the association score. Scores are represented by a colour. Cells representing associations with no evidence are coloured white.

Data type filters will also work on this view, and associations can be ordered by the association scores for individual data types by clicking the arrow head in the table header.

Tree View

The ‘Tree’ view allows visualisation of evidence across the therapeutic areas in a tree representing the relationships of diseases.

Top-level disease categories are shown at a fixed position. Clicking on a disease opens a pop-up menu that provides links to various other page, but also allows branches of the tree to be collapsed down to simplify the visualisation. Clicking on a collapsed disease will allow the branches to be opened again.

Target-Disease Evidence page

This page provides details on the evidence found to associate a target to a particular disease. The CTTV platform integrates multiple sources of information from various public resources described below.

Association score

The overall association score between a target and a disease is a numerical value indicating the strength of the association.

The value is in the range 0 to 1, with 1 indicating the strongest association.

The overall association score is produced by combining the scores for the target and disease from the individual data types (genetic associations, known drugs, etc.), and represented as a flower where each petal represents a different data type. The amount of filled colour in each petal represents the relative score within each data type. Each association data type results from the integration of one or multiple datasources. The association score for each data type depends on the confidence in each association and also on the number of associations from a particular data source.

Filtering by data type in the Target Association page won't change the overall association score. Please note that at the moment there are no negative associations listed, and the method to calculate the association score and the data source scores is temporary and will be revised in future implementations.

Data Types

Genetic associations

Genetic associations can be classified as linking to common or rare diseases:

  • The NHGRI-EBI GWAS Catalog  provides evidence on the association of targets (genes) and common diseases from published Genome Wide Association Studies (GWAS). GWAS publications are manually curated to extract lists of the primary associated variants (SNPs) by genomic region with the disease from each study. The GWAS Catalog  includes associations with p-values ≤ 10-5. Although the standard in the field for significance of an association in GWAS is for the p-value to be ≤ 5 x10-8, we provide sub-threshold values to broaden the coverage of diseases. The association is then assigned to the most likely gene by assessing the probable link between SNP and gene via a bespoke CTTV pipeline. SNPs are assigned to a gene by first considering any deleterious consequence within the genes coding region, then whether it is located within the gene's introns or regulatory regions and finally identifying the gene promoter to which it is nearest if there is no other data.
  • EBI UniProt  and the EBI European Variation Archive (EVA)  supply information on rare genetic diseases (Mendelian diseases). In this data type, evidence that a target (gene) is associated with a disease comes primarily from deleterious mutations within the protein coding region of the gene in diseased individuals and segregation of the disease and mutation within families.
  • The EBI UniProt  database is a comprehensive, high-quality and freely accessible resource of protein sequence and functional information manually curated from the literature and public databases including Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) .
  • The EBI European Variation Archive (EVA)  is an open-access database of all types of genetic variation data from human. For target - disease evidence we take clinically relevant data from EVA  where the variation is considered pathogenic, which originates primarily from the ClinVar  archive.

Clicking on the 'Browser' tab displays common disease variants and rare disease mutations associated with diseases shown on alternative transcripts (isoforms) of the gene, in their genomic context.

Somatic mutations

These mutations that may have clinical or treatment implications are extracted from the following resources:

  • The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute COSMIC  database provides evidence from somatic mutations on likely cancer drivers as manually curated in the Cancer Gene Census. The COSMIC  Cancer Gene Census is a catalogue of genes for which mutations have been causally implicated in cancer.
  • The EBI European Variation Archive (EVA)  is an open-access database of all types of genetic variation data from human. For target - disease evidence we take clinically relevant somatic mutations from EVA  where the variation is considered pathogenic, which originates primarily from the ClinVar  archive.
Known drugs

The EBI ChEMBL  database provides evidence from known drugs where the drug can be connected to a disease and a known target. ChEMBL  is an open access database that contains compound bioactivity data against drug targets. Currently, data on FDA-approved drugs (i.e. those which are approved for clinical trials in the USA) are being displayed. Clinical indication data from other countries is being reviewed and processed.

RNA expression

The EBI Expression Atlas  database provides information on gene expression patterns under different biological conditions. For CTTV, the EBI Expression Atlas  database supplies evidence from gene expression changes in studies that compare disease samples with normal samples, or in some cases with other disease samples. In addition the Expression Atlas  provides baseline expression information for each target, which can be found on the Target Profile page.

The RNA expression data table shows gene changes for which the log2 fold change is greater than 1 or less than -1 and p-value is less than 0.05 (after Benjamini & Hochberg (1995) FDR correction). The percentile rank column shows the relative rank of the gene expression fold change of that gene in comparison to all other genes for this experiment.

Biomedical literature mining

Evidence of disease associated genes can be extracted automatically by mining the up-to-date and worldwide biomedical literature corpus.

Europe PubMed Central  (Europe PMC for short) is a unique, free, information resource for biomedical and health researchers that provides access to 30.4 million+ abstracts and 3.3 million+ full text research articles from PubMed and PubMed Central. For CTTV, Europe PMC supplies evidence of links between genes and diseases by mining the titles, abstracts and full text research articles using an entity-to-entity recognition approach.

As a complement of Europe PMC text mined information, CTTV integrates also MEDLINE literature mined information from DisGeNET  via a NLP-based approach.

The Literature Mining data table shows the most relevant research articles based on the number of times an association between a gene and a disease is found across the article and highlights the relevant sentences.

Affected pathways

The EBI Reactome  database manually curates and identifies reaction pathways that are affected by pathogenic mutations.

Mouse models

The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute PhenoDigm  database provides animal models matches ranked by their phenotypic similarity to human diseases. PhenoDigm  provides evidence on associations of targets and disease via gene knockout mouse models. The orthologous human gene to the one knocked out in the mouse is identified. The phenotypic effects in the mutant mouse are then mapped to phenotypes associated with human diseases and probable matches are identified and scored.

Data Integration

Each data piece in each data type is mapped to a target term in the Experimental Factor Ontology (EFO ). The EFO  provides a systematic, machine-readable description of many components of biological systems including diseases and phenotypes. It combines parts of several biological ontologies, such as anatomy, disease and chemical compounds. The scope of EFO  is to support the annotation, analysis and visualization of data handled by many groups at the EBI and as the core ontology for the CTTV.

Disease Association page

This page provides a table view of all potential targets associated to a disease ordered by the association score. Targets can be filtered by data type and also by the reaction pathways (from Reactome ) the target belongs to.

Disease Profile page

The Disease Profile page provides a description of a disease, its synonyms and its relationship to other diseases in the Experimental Factor Ontology. This page also displays the relationships of a disease to its immediate parents and children within the EFO  hierarchy. Clicking on ‘View target associations’ links to the Disease Association page for that disease.

The Drug section on this page provides a list of all FDA-approved drugs to treat this disease.

Target Profile Page

The Target Profile page summarises all relevant information known about a particular target including synonyms, protein information from UniProt , Gene Ontology terms to describe biological processes and publications involving the target from EUROPE PubMed Central .

  • The Variants, Isoforms and genomic context section displays common disease variants and rare disease mutations associated with diseases on alternative transcripts (isoforms) of the gene.
  • Protein baseline expression displays tissue expression profiles from the Human Protein Atlas baseline expression analysis at either the protein or RNA level. Expression levels are classified as low, medium and high as described at http://www.proteinatlas.org/about/assays+annotation .
  • The RNA baseline expression information displays RNA expression levels from large scale RNA-seq profiling of normal tissues. We include data from six different studies to provide a wide coverage of different tissues. Data is provided by the EBI Expression Atlas  database.
  • The Drug section displays FDA-approved drugs that interact with the target from the ChEMBL  database. In the Target Profile page, all drugs towards this target are displayed independent of disease.
  • The Bibliography section page comes from the sources of literature data used to annotate the corresponding protein entry in EBI UniProt  and is provided through EUROPE PubMed Central .

Scoring target-disease associations

One of the aims of the CTTV platform is to allow prioritisation of targets based on their associated evidence. A scoring system has been developed to help you to answer to questions such as:

  • Which target has the most/best evidence for association with a specific disease?
  • What is the relative weight of evidence between different targets for a disease?
The score quantifies what CTTV believe to be the key factors relating to the confidence in the evidence.

Negative scores will correspond to negative evidence (although not implemented in the current version). A score of 0 corresponds to no evidence.

Current scoring scheme per datasource

The current association score between a target and a disease is the sum of its individual datatype evidence scores capped to 1. The table below summarises the current implementation. The CTTV is working on a statistical framework to improve the scoring scheme.
Datatype Score
Known Drugs
  • ChEMBL score: drug development pipeline progression score (Pre-clinical, Phase I, Phase II, Phase III, Phase IV, Approved)
RNA Expression
  • Expression Atlas score: p-value × abs(log2(fold_change)) / 10 × rank
Genetic Association
  • GWAS Catalog normalised score: target to variant mapping score × GWAS association pvalue × sample_size × severity score
  • eVA normalised score: target to variant mapping score × pathogenecity score from ClinVar
  • UniProt score: 0.5 (maybe affected by) or 1 (is affected by) depending on the curation confidence level
Somatic Mutations
  • COSMIC score: 1 (curated)
  • eVA normalised score: target to variant score × pathogenecity score from ClinVar
Literature Mining
  • Europe PMC normalised co-occurence score: title co-occurences score + abstract co-occurences score + full text co-occurences score
  • DisGeNET co-occurence score: publication 
Affected Pathways
  • Reactome score: 1 (curated)
Animal Models
  • Phenodigm similarity score: publication
