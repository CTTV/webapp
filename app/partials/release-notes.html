Release 0.1 (release 2015-03-02)

Status

This version is the first release of the CTTV platform. The following major features were added:

  • search box: you can retrieve gene of interest by entering gene symbol, gene name and gene synonym
  • search box: you can also search by disease name provided the disease label and its synonyms are part of the Experimental Factor Ontology (EFO)
  • search results: results of a search are presented by order of relevance based on the matching of symbol, names and synonyms. Matched terms are highlighted
  • search filters: search results can be filtered by gene and/or disease. Number of matching genes and number of matching diseases are shown
  • auto-completion box: search terms are auto-completed. the auto-completion presents - up to 3 - matched gene and disease terms
  • auto-completion box: disease ontology classification is presented under the disease term and clickable. A disease hyperlink links to the disease association page
  • auto-completion box: the hyperlink of an auto-completed gene links to a gene associations page for this gene
  • disease associations page: provides an overview of all diseases associated to a target. 2 views are available: a 'bubble view' and a 'tabular view'
  • disease associations bubble view: groups disease by therapeutic area into bubbles. Because of the EFO ontology, a disease can belong to multiple therapeutic area
  • disease associations bubble view: you can zoom in a particular therapeutic area by clicking on the corresponding therapeutic area filter
  • disease associations bubble view: you can zoom out of a particular therapeutic area by clicking on the corresponding therapeutic area filter again
  • disease associations bubble view: you can click on a bubble to get the association score between a specific disease and a target. This score is broken down by data types and represented as a flower
  • disease associations bubble view: you can go to a detailed gene-disease page by following the view details link when clicking on a bubble
  • disease associations tabular view: lists all diseases associated to a particular gene ordered by association score. This score is broken down by data types and represented as a flower
  • association score flower: represents the relative contribution of each data type when scoring a gene to disease association
  • gene to disease association page: provides details on the evidence found to associate a particular gene to a particular disease (from EFO)
  • gene to disease association page: genetic associations from eVA, UniProt and the GWAS catalog are presented
  • gene to disease association page: somatic mutations from the cancer gene census COSMIC resource are presented
  • gene to disease association page: known drugs from ChEMBL are presented
  • gene to disease association page: RNA expression information from the Expression Atlas are presented
  • gene to disease association page: disrupted pathways from Reactome are presented
  • gene to disease association page: mouse models from PhenoDigm are presented
  • target profile page: summarises all information known about a particular target including synonyms, UniProt and Ensembl information, GO terms, baseline expression and list of publications from EUROPE PubMed Central
  • disease profile page: provides a description of a disease, its synonyms and its classification in the Experimental Factor Ontology
  • target association page: provides a table view of all potential targets associated to a disease as a table
  • target associations tabular view: lists all targets associated to a particular disease ordered by association score. This score is broken down by data types and represented as a flower

The CTTV platform integrates multiple source of information from the following public resources:

  • UniProt: a comprehensive, high-quality and freely accessible resource of protein sequence and functional information
  • European Variation Archive (EVA): an open-access database of all types of genetic variation data from human
  • GWAS Catalog: the NHGRI-EBI Catalog of published genome-wide association studies
